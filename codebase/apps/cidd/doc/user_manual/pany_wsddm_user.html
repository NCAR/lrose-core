1.1 CIDD's Basic Layout Back to top

CIDD consistes of two principle display panels; one of plan view and one for cross sectional data views. In addition a set of control sub panels are used to select products, add or change attributes of products in the images, control movie looping, etc. Many of the most frequently used operations such as selecting different products, heights or time of interest can be performed with one keystoke or mouse click in the display window, or popup panels. Side by side comparisions of plan view or cross sectional data are accomplished by running multiple instances of CIDD. There is a CLone button/function available in the menu bar.

CIDD's Menu BarBack to top

The main display window of CIDD has a menu bar area above the plan view graphics. This menu bar is configurable; 23 different functinoal buttons can be independantly ensbled or disabled and their labels chosen. (Via the Menu bar configuration section in the parameter file). Thus, the apperance and labels of the menu bar change from project to project. Menus for a typical end-user display are used in this document.
The menu bar buttons operate in two basic ways;
  • 1. The first type activates/ deactivates pop-up panels or menus(Example ), which provide for additional controls. In some cases, the menu will automatically de-select itself once a coice has been made. Otherwise, the button acts as a toggle for displaying the subpanel.
  • 2. The second type is a feature initiator or toggle. They activate a function or toggle something on or off like movie looping.
Figure 1.1 shows the CIDD main window in detail. Along the top of the main window is a menu bar

Directly below the menu bar is the time and flight level information bar. This bar shows:

  • Data time and frame sequence: the frame, date and time selected,
  • Product flight level: flight level for the product displayed
  • Current date and time: UTC

The margins contain the following:

  • Latitude scale: on the left side of the window
  • Longitude scale: on the top of the window
  • Main product info: displays the date and time of the product being displayed
  • Model date and time for the forecast winds: The text in the upper left corner of the main window indicates the date and time of the main data field selected. If wind vectors are on, the date and time of the wind data is also shown. (Date and time of the forecast wind vectors being displayed.)
  • Time scale: on the bottom of the screen - Color scale: next to the flight level bar on the right side of the main window. The units of the data field are indicated at the top of the color scale. - Height scale: on the right side of the screen.

Changing Height/Elevation Back to top

To change the elevation level, click the left mouse button in the height margin. You can also step up and down in height by pressing the up or down arrow key on the keyboard
Note: for this to work, the pointer must be within the main display window.

Changing TimeBack to top

To change the time, click in the time scale bar. To return to real time (i.e., the time closest to the current time), click on the 'Now' button in the main menu bar at the top. Pressing the left or right arrow keys on the keyboard also moves the time by one frame
Note: for this to work, the pointer must be within the main display window.

Zooming and PanningBack to top

The view menu allows you to adjust the zoom state to pre-defined domains. There are also buttons that allow you to save custom zoom states and return to the saved states.

Using Zoom¡GUse the click and drag method for zooming (with the left-mouse button) to get a user-defined region. This user-defined region becomes the current zoom and will cause the 'Current' button to be selected. Unzoom by choosing one of the pre-defined domains.

Panning¡GYou can pan or move the image with the middle mouse button: click on a point and drag it to the desired location, then release.

Saving zoom states¡GTo save the current zoom, click on 'Save 1', 'Save 2', or 'Save 3'. To go back to the saved view, click on 'Goto 1', 'Goto 2', or 'Goto 3'.

After all frames have been rendered, the display will cycle rapidly through the frames with a delay at the end of the loop.

5.3.6.13 Route ButtonBack to top
Please refer to Fig. 5.3.6.13-1, Fig. 5.3.6.13-2 and Fig. 5.3.6.13-3 on following pages.

Thebutton toggles the flight route vertical cross-section display on/off. You select the desired route by clicking and dragging, using the right mouse button. Multiple segments may be chosen. Release the button at each way point, and then click and drag to the next way point. If you make a mistake, click the left button to erase the previous segment. Double click on the end of the last segment to complete the selection.

The terrain along the route is depicted in solid brown, overlaying the data field. To change to a different field, right-click in the vertical section window and select the field from the menu that appears. The flight level scale appears in the left margin.

Fig. 5.3.6.13-1 shows a user-selected route in plan view. The route is shown as a bold yellow line. The waypoints are labeled by the closest navigational fix (way point, VHF navaid or airport) within 20 nm of the selected point. If no navigational fix lies within a 20 nm radius, the point will be marked with a number indicating its position on the route. For example, the first point will be labeled "1", the third point "3", and so on.

Figure 5.3.6.13-2 shows the corresponding vertical section. The selected field (in this case turbulence probability) is shown with the color scale legend on the right. As mentioned above, you may select a route by clicking and dragging in plan view. You may also select a route from a pre-defined list of routes.

On Figure 5.3.5.13-2, you will see the button marked 'Routes ...'. Clicking on this button will bring up a list of pre-defined routes. You may click on any of these to select it. The latest route you entered by clicking and dragging may be retrieved by using the 'Custom' button on that menu.

The 'Label Waypoints' is one of the check-boxes that toggle the waypoint labels on and off, in case they are obscuring data. If waypoint labels is on, the navigational fix lies within a 20 nm radius will be marked. Otherwise, they will not.

The 'Show Winds Text' is the other check-box that toggles the segment route with values on and off. See Figure 5.3.6.13-3 for an example with the route winds on. The mean wind for segments along the flight is shown plotted at the mid-point of the segment.

The 'Average wind' label located directly under the window title bar displays the average wind along the entire flight route.

The 'Turbulence and/or icing warnings' label located under 'Average wind' label, in red, warns if a route goes through an area of turbulence or icing, or both. Otherwise it is empty. (In the example, it shows "Mod Turb" indicating the route passes through an area with a moderate probability of turbulence.)

The selected flight level is depicted by the dashed horizontal line.

The distance along the route in nautical miles is shown along the bottom axis, with the starting point on the left.

The waypoints are plotted as vertical dashed lines, with the waypoint name at the bottom. (The navigational fix lies within a 20 nm radius will be marked.)

Use the 'Dismiss' button to remove the vertical section from the screen. Toggling thebutton on the main menu may also be used for this purpose.

6. Tables and GraphsBack to top

  1. AsciiSelect - Displays Raw ASCII data nearest the click point - (METARS, TAFS, PIREPS, etc)

    5.8 TAFs Text Window Back to top

    Refer to Figure 5.8-1. o bring this window up, click on theicon. The window will show the TAFs for the stations closest to the last point clicked on the main display window. The TAFs are in time-reverse order. To update the window, click on the airport of interest.

  2. MetarSelect - Displays Decoded Metars nearest the click point

    5.4 Decoded METAR Text Window Back to top

    Refer to Figure 5.4-1. The decoded METAR text program (MetarSelect) displays metar data, sorted by distance from the click point. If configured for real-time operation, the program will display the latest data available. In archive mode, the program will use information from CIDD to obtain the time of interest and will gather data appropriate for that time.

    To update the window, select the desired METAR station by clicking in the main display window close to the METAR station of interest. The METAR text window will be updated with the latest reporting METAR information for that station and nearby stations. The closest station will appear at the top of the list. The data is in time-reverse order with the latest observations at the top.

    You can change the size of the font by holding down the control key, putting the cursor in the window and then clicking the right mouse button. A font menu will appear. Select the desired font. Select the 'Default' option to use the original font.

  3. StationStripChart - Displays a time history of Surface Station Data.

    5.10 METAR Time History WindowBack to top

    Refer to Figure 5.10-1 This program shows the time history for METAR stations. To change the field, right-click and select the desired field in the pop-up menu that appears. The active fields are: temperature, humidity, wind speed, pressure and wind direction. Alternatively, you can select a station of interest in the pop-up menu, which shows all the fields for that station.

  4. pos_report - A position Reporting tool. Gives distance and azmith relative to pre-configured points.
  5. snow_plot - Displays time histories and forcasts for Radar reflectivity and Surface station data precip rates
  6. time-hist - Displays graphs of TITAN Storm characteristics.
  7. mesonet_text_report - Displays Textual Surface station data in a simple form.
  8. winds_aloft - Displays vertical column of Winds, Temps etc above click point.

    5.5 Winds/Temperature Aloft WindowBack to Top

    Refer to Figure 5.5-1. The winds/temps aloft window appears in the middle of the screen at the right hand side.

    To update the window, click in the main window at the point of interest. The text in the winds/temps aloft window will show a vertical profile of the forecasted (MM5 model) wind speed, direction, temperature, dew-point and relative humidity for the location at which you clicked. The time on the panel is the forecast valid time. To change the forecast time, select a different time on the time scale bar in the main window. The location (Lat, Lon) of the vertical profile is also provided in the widow. Also, the name of the closest navigational point within 50 nm is printed below the lat/lon location. Data will not be displayed if you click outside the MM5 data area.

    Occasionally you will see data fields containing "xxxx" instead of a number. This indicates missing data for that field and is usually caused by the fact that the relevant flight level is below the terrain at the selected point.
